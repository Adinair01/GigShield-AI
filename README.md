# GigShield AI  
### Parametric Income Protection Platform for Gig Workers

---

## Overview

Gig workers power modern on-demand services such as food delivery, ride sharing, and quick commerce. However, their earnings depend entirely on their ability to operate every day.

External factors like **heavy rainfall, flooding, or extreme pollution** can suddenly disrupt their ability to work. Unlike salaried employees, gig workers often have **no financial protection against these disruptions**.

GigShield AI explores a new approach to this problem: **parametric income protection**, where payouts are triggered automatically based on environmental conditions rather than traditional claim processing.

This repository contains the **Phase-1 architecture and foundational prototype** of the GigShield platform.

---

## Problem Statement

Gig workers frequently face interruptions caused by environmental conditions that are outside their control.

### Heavy Rainfall
Delivery partners may be unable to complete deliveries during severe rainfall because roads become unsafe and demand drops.

### Flood Risk
Flood alerts in certain city zones can temporarily stop gig platform operations in that area.

### Extreme Pollution
Outdoor gig workers face serious health risks during severe air pollution, but stopping work means losing income.

Traditional insurance products are not designed to handle **short-term income disruptions** that gig workers experience.

---

## Proposed Solution

GigShield AI introduces a **parametric protection model** for gig workers.

Instead of requiring workers to manually submit claims, the system monitors environmental conditions and evaluates predefined trigger thresholds.

Examples of triggers include:

- Rainfall exceeding a specific level  
- Flood alerts in delivery zones  
- Hazardous air quality levels  

When these triggers occur, the system automatically evaluates whether workers operating in that zone qualify for compensation.

This approach enables:

- Faster payouts  
- Reduced claim processing  
- Transparent trigger-based coverage

---

## Persona-Based Scenario

### Persona

Ravi is a food delivery partner working in Bengaluru.

He subscribes to GigShield by paying a **small weekly premium** that provides income protection against environmental disruptions.

### Situation

During the monsoon season, rainfall in Ravi's delivery zone exceeds the defined threshold.

The system detects this environmental trigger and identifies workers operating in that area.

### System Workflow

1. Environmental data crosses trigger threshold  
2. Backend identifies workers operating in the affected zone  
3. Risk scoring model evaluates disruption severity  
4. Eligible workers are flagged for payout evaluation  

This allows the system to provide **automatic and transparent protection** without requiring manual claims.

---

## Weekly Premium Model

GigShield uses a lightweight weekly subscription model suitable for gig workers.

Example structure:

- Worker enrolls in a weekly protection plan  
- Coverage applies for the next 7 days  
- Environmental triggers during this period activate payout evaluation  

Risk scores generated by the AI model help determine appropriate premium levels depending on city-level environmental risks.

---

## Role of AI / Machine Learning

AI is used to assist in evaluating environmental risk and improving system intelligence.

### Risk Scoring

A machine learning model estimates environmental disruption risk using inputs such as:

- Historical rainfall patterns  
- Pollution index levels  
- Flood risk probability  

The output is a **risk score** used by the backend service to evaluate disruption severity.

### Fraud Detection (Future Work)

Future phases will introduce AI models that detect suspicious claim patterns, including:

- Repeated claims from the same location  
- Environmental inconsistencies  
- Unusual claim frequency
